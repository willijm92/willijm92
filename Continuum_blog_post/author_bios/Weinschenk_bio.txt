Craig Weinschenk is an engineer in the Fire Fighting Technology Group of the Fire Research Division (FRD) of the Engineering Laboratory at the National Institute of Standards and Technology.

Craig joined the FRD in August of 2011 to research issues related to modeling carbon monoxide production in under-ventilated compartment fires. Craig has also worked on firefighter safety projects including a staffing study focused on determining the appropriate response to high-rise fires and computer modeling of firefighter line of duty deaths. Craig is currently conducting research in the areas of fire fighting tactics, heat transfer in firefighter protective clothing, numerical combustion, and fire-structure interaction modeling.
